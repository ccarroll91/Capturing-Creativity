What do they create?

In a word, art.
There is a lot that can be defined as art, and the people here are involved in what we call Small Press. They're not working with publishers, although that might be the aim for some. They work hard at their craft to help bring new and exciting stories to the world.





They make comics (comicbooks) and zines (like a short magazine). It is visual storytelling at its finest.



They create fun, and cater to so many genres.



Who makes a comic?

Writer: They write the script. Everything from the story, descriptions of the scene, and the dialogue.

Artist: They draw the comic. Some people opt for pencils, requiring an additional inker to step, but generally the artists in Small Press will do both. Additionally, there may be a seperate Cover Artist whose role it is to draw the cover of the book.

Colourist: They add the colours to a comic page, bringing the lines of the artist to life. Some colourists will work with a Flatter who applies the base layer of colours to the comic page, preparing the page for additional colours.

Letterer: An often underappreciated role, the letter does so much more than add in speech bubbles. A good letterer will not just let the reader know what's being said, but they'll make them feel like they are in the page.

Editor: The Editor will ensure that what's in the script actually appears on the page, ensuring that an artist doesn't go rogue and draw what they feel like.

Designer: A Designer will help a team make their book look like a book.
